Most advisory relationships begin too late — after the drift has set in, the decisions have been delayed, the window has narrowed. I enter earlier.

I bring operational judgement, not strategic opinion. The difference is material: I have built businesses, navigated governance under pressure, managed complex integrations, and led through the kind of organisational difficulty that frameworks alone cannot resolve.

I work with founders, PE-backed leadership teams, boards and investors who need more than a well-structured presentation. They need someone who has been in the room — and who isn't afraid to say what they see.

A Framework for Stalled Businesses

The Hesitation
Gap.

One of the most consistent patterns in businesses under pressure is not a lack of strategic clarity — it is the gap between understanding what must change and the willingness to act on it.

Read the full framework

"When leaders have strategic clarity but delay decisive action — creating controlled drift until forced action becomes the only option."

The Hesitation Gap describes the period in which a business knows what it must do but remains held back by relational, structural or identity-entangled forces. The longer the gap, the narrower the window. Second Curve is designed to close it.
